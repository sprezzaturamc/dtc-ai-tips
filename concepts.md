# How AI works

Learning AI isn't complicated — it just takes practice. A little understanding of the machinery makes every tip on this site land harder.

## Patterns and tokens

Can u raed tihs? If so, it's because human language is full of patterns. Large Language Models (LLMs) are trained on trillions of **tokens** (chunks about the size of a word) to detect those patterns across every digital medium.

When you send a prompt, the model is filling in the pattern your prompt began — choosing the next most probable token, one at a time. *The more you specify, the less it has to guess.* That single idea is what all prompt and context engineering comes down to.

## What that means for you

If the model has seen most of the digital corpus, then with the right prompt **you can produce almost anything you can clearly describe**. The real limits are:

1. Your ability to dream big.
2. Your ability to evaluate the result.

## Why it sometimes makes things up

LLMs are powerful because they *infer* — they fill gaps. That requires fuzziness, and the fuzziness is built into the math. It's why models sometimes **hallucinate** even with precise instructions. It's not a bug you can fully prompt away; it's how the tool works. So check anything that matters.

## A few terms

- **Token** — a chunk of text the model reads and writes, roughly a word.
- **Context** — everything you give the model in one go: prompt, pasted data, images, prior turns.
- **Context engineering** — managing that context to raise the odds of the answer you want.
- **Hallucination** — a confident answer that isn't true; verify important facts.
- **System prompt** — standing instructions that shape how the model behaves before your message.
